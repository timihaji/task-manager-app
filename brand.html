The story

You delegate a task. For the first day, you remember. By the third day, your brain has filed it under done — even though it isn't. Two weeks later, someone asks, "hey, whatever happened to…" and your stomach drops.

Echo is built on a simple observation: the dangerous thing is silence. When work leaves your hands, you stop hearing about it — and most task managers don't notice. They treat assigned as the end of the story.

Echo treats it as the beginning. Every delegation has an expected rhythm of response: a daily check-in for the contractor, a weekly sync with a teammate, a monthly review with a vendor. When that rhythm breaks, Echo surfaces the silence — quietly, before it becomes a fire.

You don't have to remember to follow up. You don't have to micromanage. You don't have to keep a private spreadsheet of who owes you what. Echo listens, and tells you when it's time to ask.

Message pillars

Silence is the signal.

Most apps tell you when something is done. Echo tells you when something has gone too long without an update.

You're the one on the hook.

Accountability stays with the delegator. Echo is built around you, not the doer.

Cadence, not chasing.

Set the rhythm once — daily, weekly, every other Thursday. Echo handles the rest. No nag-the-team mode.

People memory.

Echo remembers who you've delegated to before, what worked, what stalled — so the next handoff starts smarter.

Tone of voice

Do

  • Calm. The opposite of urgency-bait.
  • Plain-spoken. "Handoff," not "workstream."
  • Slightly knowing — we've both been the one who forgot to follow up.
  • Confident in the small claim ("we surface stale handoffs").
  • Quiet specifics over loud generalities.

Don't

  • "Revolutionize your team."
  • "Unlock your potential."
  • Synergize. Leverage. Workstream. Holistic.
  • Exclamation points. Hype.
  • Pretend Echo replaces 1:1s or trust.

Visual direction

Motif

Concentric ripples — a dot with rings propagating outward. The mark is the product: a single point that sends a signal and waits for a reply. Use sparingly; the static mark is one ring + one dot, the animated version (loading states, empty-state illustrations) shows the propagation.

Type

Listens for the silence.
Headlines and brand voice use Source Serif (or Tiempos / GT Alpina in production) — a quiet, considered serif. UI and body copy use Geist, the existing app sans. Mono is Geist Mono for code & meta.
Source Serif 4 · Geist · Geist Mono

Iconography

Line-weight, slightly hand-drawn. Less geometric than Linear, less playful than Trello. The Echo mark itself sits at the centre of the system — every loading state, every empty state, every "waiting on someone" surface can echo the ripple.

Backup: Polaris

If Echo runs into trademark or domain trouble

Polaris flips the metaphor: instead of listening for silence, it's the fixed point in a shifting sky. Tagline: "The star that doesn't move."

Same product, slightly more contemplative tone. Caveat: there's a small Japanese todo app already on Google Play called Polaris, and Polaris Office competes for SEO. Use only if Echo is blocked.

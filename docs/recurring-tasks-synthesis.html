Recurring tasks · synthesis

Tasks and Routines: same data model, two UX treatments.

"Same or different?" hides the real question. The lever isn't task-vs-routine — it's cadence frequency. Build one model, treat it two ways, and never ask the user to choose at creation time.

1The two extremes both fail

Most apps pick one side. Each side has a specific failure mode.

"They're the same thing"
Sunsama · Todoist · Things 3

A routine is just a task with a recurrence rule. One concept, one input flow, one view.

you come back from a one-week holiday to 7 overdue dog walks, 5 missed journals, and 14 ghost "stand up at 9am"s clogging your inbox. The signal — real overdue tasks like rent — drowns in routine noise.
"They're different things"
Streaks · Habitica · Habit-tracking apps

Habits live in their own world with their own UI, their own streaks, their own data store.

you now maintain two task systems. "Was that a habit or a task?" becomes a daily question. Cross-cutting actions (search, filters, projects, tags) don't span both. Migration nightmares.

2The real lever is cadence frequency

Every "real" difference between a task and a routine correlates with one thing: how often it repeats.

Cadence frequency

dailyweekdays3× / wkweeklymonthlyquarterly
↻ Routine treatment
walk dog · daily journal · weekly review · stretch
✓ Task-with-cadence treatment
pay rent · submit invoice · quarterly review · car service

Above the line: the series matters, individual instances don't. Missing one is noise. Streak is the truth.

Below the line: each instance matters. Missing one is a real debt. The card stays overdue until handled.

3The position

One data model (tasks with recurrence), one flag (isRoutine, auto-derived from cadence), two UX treatments that diverge at exactly three points: rollover, view treatment, and streaks.

No new table. No new top-level concept. No "task or routine?" question when you create something. The system makes the call from cadence; you override with one toggle if it's wrong.

6The input flow

One chip row, one auto-toggle. The user never picks "task or routine?" — they pick a cadence; the system picks the treatment.

Repeats

Daily Weekdays Mon/Wed/Fri Weekly Monthly · Nth Custom…
Treat as routine — rolls forward silently, tracks streak
Auto-on for daily / weekdays / 3+ days a week. Turn off to keep missed instances as overdue.
If you pick Weekdays → auto-on. If you pick Monthly → auto-off. One click to override.

7The one schema change

// src/data.js — makeTask
recurrence: { freq, interval, byDay, until },
isRoutine: false, // auto-derived; user can override
recurrenceId: null, // shared across all instances of a series

recurrenceId is the only structurally important addition — it makes streak math O(series-length) instead of O(all-tasks). Assigned when recurrence is first set; copied on every spawn.

10The honest tradeoff

Where the auto-flag guesses wrong

An auto-derived isRoutine will be wrong ~5% of the time. Someone wants their monthly meditation tracked as a streak. Someone else wants their daily admin review kept as a hard overdue task. The override toggle handles both, but the first time it surprises someone they'll grumble.

The alternative — asking "is this a task or a routine?" at every creation — surprises them every time. The toggle's the better cost.
